# Provenance Guard — Planning

## Milestone 1

### 1. Architecture Narrative

When someone submits text to `POST /submit`, the Flask app takes the raw text and passes it to two detection signals. The first signal sends the text to Groq's `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` model and asks it to judge whether the text sounds human-written or AI-generated. The second signal runs a stylometric checker written in plain Python, which looks at things like sentence length variance, type-token ratio, and punctuation density. Both signals return a score. A confidence scorer combines these two scores into one overall number, showing how sure the system is about its call. That combined score gets turned into a transparency label — a short, human-readable tag like "Likely AI-Generated" or "Likely Human-Written" plus the confidence level. Before sending anything back, the app writes an entry to the audit log, recording the submission, both signal scores, the combined score, and the label. Finally, the app sends the response back to the user with the label and confidence score attached.

### 2. Detection Signals

**Signal 1: LLM-based classification (Groq / llama-3.3-70b-versatile)**
- **What it measures:** It asks a large language model to read the text and give its opinion on whether the writing sounds human or AI-generated, based on patterns it has learned from huge amounts of text.
- **Why it differs between AI and human writing:** AI-generated text tends to be more even, predictable, and "smooth," while human writing often has quirks, personal voice, and small mistakes that an LLM has learned to recognize as human signals.
- **Blind spot:** It can be fooled by AI text that has been edited by a human, or by human text that happens to be very polished and formal — the model is guessing based on style, not proof.

**Signal 2: Stylometric heuristics (pure Python)**
- **What it measures:** Simple math-based patterns in the text — how much sentence lengths vary, the ratio of unique words to total words (type-token ratio), and how often punctuation shows up.
- **Why it differs between AI and human writing:** Human writers naturally vary their sentence length and word choice more, while AI text often has steadier rhythm and more repeated word patterns. Punctuation habits can also differ.
- **Blind spot:** It can't understand meaning or context, so a human writer with a very consistent style (or an AI told to "write more randomly") can easily slip past this check.

### 3. False-Positive Trace

Say a human writer submits an essay, but the system flags it as AI-generated by mistake. Here's how that should play out:

1. **Detection:** Signal 1 (the LLM) might lean toward "AI-generated" because the writing is clean and well-structured. Signal 2 (stylometry) might disagree, showing normal human-like variation in sentence length and word choice.
2. **Confidence scoring:** Because the two signals disagree, the combined confidence score should come out low or "medium" — not a strong, confident call. The scorer should be built so that disagreement between signals always pulls the confidence down, not up.
3. **Transparency label:** Instead of a firm label like "AI-Generated," the system should show something more careful, like "Possibly AI-Generated (Low Confidence)" — making it clear this isn't a certain result.
4. **Appeal:** The human writer can then use `POST /appeal` to challenge the label. This creates an appeal record tied to the original submission, updates its status to "under review" (or similar), and logs the appeal in the audit trail. A reviewer (or a future automated re-check) can then update the status to "overturned" or "upheld," and that decision also gets logged.

### 4. API Surface + Diagram

**Endpoints**

- **POST /submit**
  - Input: `{ "text": "..." }`
  - Output: `{ "submission_id": "...", "label": "...", "confidence": 0.0-1.0, "signal_scores": { "llm": ..., "stylometry": ... } }`

- **POST /appeal**
  - Input: `{ "submission_id": "...", "reason": "..." }`
  - Output: `{ "appeal_id": "...", "status": "under_review" }`

- **GET /log**
  - Input: none (maybe optional query filters later)
  - Output: `{ "entries": [ { "submission_id": "...", "label": "...", "confidence": ..., "timestamp": "..." }, ... ] }`
